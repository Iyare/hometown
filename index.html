About

Benin City is the capital of Edo State in southern Nigeria. It is a city approximately 25 miles north of the Benin River. It is situated 200 miles by road east of Lagos. Benin City is the centre of Nigeria's rubber industry, but processing palm nuts for oil is still an important traditional industry. The city is also the home of the University of Benin. The people of Benin City are known for their bronze casting, wood carving and other crafts. The Oba of Benin is the traditional ruler of the Edo people and all Edos respect the Oba. The Oba of Benin's Palace is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The Benin Bronzes are a group of plaques and sculptures that decorated the Royal Palace of Benin City. The Benin Bronzes are now displayed in various museums around the world. The Benin Bronzes are a reminder of the rich history and culture of the Edo people.

Thought Pyramid Art Center

It's a hub for contemporary art and culture. It aims to promote and preserve African art by providing a platform for artists to showcase their work. The center hosts various exhibitions, workshops, and events that engage the local community and visitors alike. It is a place where art enthusiasts can explore the rich artistic heritage of Benin City and discover new and emerging talents. The Thought Pyramid Art Center is dedicated to fostering creativity and supporting the growth of the art scene in Nigeria.

Kada Cinemas

Spend some time with friends and family at the Kada Cinemas. This spot offers a modern and comfortable movie-going experience with state-of-the-art facilities. The cinema features multiple screens showing the latest blockbuster movies, both local and international. In addition to movies, Kada Cinemas also hosts special events and screenings, making it a popular spot for both residents and visitors. The cinema is part of the larger Kada Plaza, which includes a variety of shops, restaurants, and recreational activities, providing a complete entertainment experience for the whole family.

Okomu National Park

Okomu National Park is a protected area located in the Ovia South-West Local Government Area of Edo State, Nigeria. It is one of the last remaining rainforests in Nigeria and covers an area of approximately 200 square kilometers. The park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including endangered species such as the white-throated monkey, forest elephants, and the Nigerian-Cameroon chimpanzee. Visitors to Okomu National Park can enjoy activities such as bird watching, hiking, and guided tours through the lush forest. The park also features a canopy walkway that offers a unique perspective of the forest from above.
